Simple Interest

Interest calculated only on the original amount – straightforward, predictable.

Simple Interest in one minute

You lend ₹10,000 at 10% per year for 3 years. Every year interest = ₹1,000. Total interest in 3 years = ₹3,000. Interest is always on the original ₹10,000, not growing. That is simple interest.

A little deeper

Formula

SI = Principal Ö Rate Ö Time ÷ 100. Clean and predictable.

Where used

Some vehicle loans, certain fixed deposits, money market instruments use simple interest.

SI vs CI

Simple interest does not grow your interest. Compound interest does. Over long periods, CI is far superior for the investor.

Borrower preference

As a borrower, you prefer simple interest – you pay less over time.

How people making money from this

Short-term loans

Short-term personal loans, bridge loans often use simple interest. Simpler to calculate repayment.

Treasury bills

Short-term government securities use simple interest calculations.

Risk

Simple interest deposits give you less than compound interest deposits over the same period. Choose wisely.
