ROI (Return On Investment)

How much money did you earn back for every rupee you put in?

ROI in one minute

You invested ₹10,000 in a mutual fund. After one year you have ₹12,000. You made ₹2,000 profit. ROI = ₹2,000 ÷ ₹10,000 Ö 100 = 20%. For every ₹100 invested, you got ₹20 back. Simple.

A little deeper

Formula

ROI = (Final Value − Initial Investment) ÷ Initial Investment Ö 100

Compare investments

FD gave 6% ROI. Mutual fund gave 14% ROI. Clearly mutual fund won – for the same risk level.

Time matters

20% ROI over 1 year is great. 20% ROI over 10 years is poor. Always compare time period.

Risk-adjusted ROI

Higher ROI with lower risk = better investment. Do not chase ROI without considering risk.

How people making money from this

Use for all decisions

House renovation ROI. Business investment ROI. Education ROI. ROI thinking helps everywhere.

CAGR is better

For multi-year investments, use CAGR (compound annual growth rate) instead of simple ROI.

Risk

Past ROI does not guarantee future returns. Always factor in risk before comparing.
