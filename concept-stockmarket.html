Stock Market

The big marketplace where shares of thousands of companies are bought and sold every day

Stock Market in one minute

Think of it like a giant vegetable market. But instead of vegetables, people buy and sell ownership slices (shares) of companies. Prices change every second based on how many people want to buy or sell. That is the stock market.

A little deeper

How prices move

More buyers than sellers = price goes up. More sellers than buyers = price goes down. It is pure demand and supply.

BSE & NSE

India has two main stock exchanges – BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange) and NSE (National Stock Exchange). Most big companies are listed on both.

Trading hours

Indian markets are open Mon–Fri, 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM. Prices change every second during this time.

Who can invest?

Anyone with a PAN card, bank account, and Demat account can invest in Indian stock markets.

How people making money from this

Buying low, selling high

Buy shares when price is low. Sell when price rises. The difference is your profit.

Long term vs short term

Long-term investors hold for years. Short-term traders buy and sell quickly. Long term is safer for beginners.

Risks

Markets crash sometimes. In 2008, markets fell 60%. They recovered later. But short-term investors can panic-sell and lose.
